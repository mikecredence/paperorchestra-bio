## Working title

ARHGAP18-Ezrin as a RhoA autoregulatory module organizing apical microvilli

## Core question

How is RhoA locally regulated to support two distinct adjacent actin structures -- microvilli and the terminal web -- on the apical surface of epithelial cells?

## Motivation / gap

- The apical surface of epithelial cells has microvilli (ezrin-dependent) directly adjacent to the terminal web (myosin-2-dependent), both requiring active RhoA
- How RhoA activity is spatially partitioned between these two actin structures is unknown
- ARHGAP18 is a RhoGAP with no established role in apical polarity or microvillar organization

## Core contribution

- Show that ARHGAP18 localizes to microvilli by binding active (phosphorylated) ezrin
- Demonstrate that binding to active ezrin enhances ARHGAP18's RhoGAP activity
- Propose a negative autoregulatory model: RhoA activates ezrin, active ezrin recruits ARHGAP18, ARHGAP18 locally reduces RhoA -- creating a self-limiting circuit
- Show that loss of ARHGAP18 disrupts the boundary between microvilli and terminal web, with aberrant myosin-2 filament assembly inside microvilli

## Method in brief

- Epithelial cell lines (Caco-2, Jeg-3, LLCPK) expressing tagged constructs
- Super-resolution and confocal microscopy of microvillar and terminal web markers
- Co-immunoprecipitation and pulldown assays (ARHGAP18 - ezrin interaction)
- RhoGAP activity assays (in vitro GTPase stimulation)
- CRISPR/siRNA knockdown of ARHGAP18
- Structured illumination microscopy (SIM) for myosin-2 filament localization
