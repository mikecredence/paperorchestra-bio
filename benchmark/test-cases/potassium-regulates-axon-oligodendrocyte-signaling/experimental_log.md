# Experimental Log: K+ Regulation of Axon-OL Metabolic Coupling

## Experiment 1: OL calcium responses to axonal stimulation

Two-photon Ca2+ imaging in optic nerve oligodendrocytes.

| Stimulus Condition | Peak Ca2+ (dF/F) | Latency (s) | n (cells) | p-value vs baseline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline (no stim) | 0.02 | -- | 45 | -- |
| Low-freq axonal firing (10 Hz) | 0.18 | 2.1 | 42 | <0.01 |
| High-freq axonal firing (100 Hz) | 0.65 | 0.8 | 48 | <0.001 |
| Extracellular K+ (10 mM) | 0.58 | 0.6 | 40 | <0.001 |
| High-freq + barium (Kir blocker) | 0.12 | 4.5 | 38 | 0.15 (vs baseline) |

## Experiment 2: Transporter expression in Kir4.1 cKO vs control

## Experiment 3: Axonal integrity over time

Compound action potential (CAP) amplitude in optic nerve.

| Age | Control CAP (mV) | Kir4.1 cKO CAP (mV) | % Reduction | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P30 | 1.85 | 1.72 | 7% | 0.12 |
| P60 | 1.80 | 1.35 | 25% | <0.01 |
| P120 | 1.78 | 0.95 | 47% | <0.001 |

## Notes

- OL Ca2+ response is fast and K+-dependent via Kir4.1 channels.
- Transporter downregulation (GLUT1/MCT1) in Kir4.1 cKO occurs before detectable axon loss.
- Progressive axonal degeneration in Kir4.1 cKO consistent with metabolic starvation model.
