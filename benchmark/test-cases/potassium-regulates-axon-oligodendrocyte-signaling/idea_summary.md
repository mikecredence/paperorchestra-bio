## Working title

Potassium Regulates Axon-Oligodendrocyte Signaling and Metabolic Coupling in White Matter

## Core question

How do oligodendrocytes detect axonal spiking activity and rapidly control metabolic support to myelinated axons, and what role does potassium play in this coupling?

## Motivation / gap

- Oligodendrocytes provide essential homeostatic support to myelinated axons, but the signaling mechanism linking axonal activity to OL metabolic response is largely unknown.
- It was unclear whether extracellular K+ released during axonal firing could serve as the activity-dependent signal that triggers OL calcium responses and metabolic coupling.
- The functional consequences of disrupting oligodendroglial Kir4.1 channels on axonal energy metabolism and long-term axon integrity had not been characterized.

## Core contribution

- Show that high-frequency axonal firing and extracellular K+ elevations trigger fast Ca2+ responses in oligodendrocytes via barium-sensitive inwardly rectifying K+ channels
- Demonstrate using OL-specific Kir4.1 knockout mice (Kir4.1 cKO) that Kir4.1 regulates axonal energy metabolism and long-term axonal integrity
- Reveal that reduced GLUT1 and MCT1 transporter expression in myelin of young Kir4.1 cKO mice precedes axonal damage
- Establish K+ as a key activity-dependent signal for axon-OL metabolic coupling

## Method in brief

- Optic nerve electrophysiology combined with two-photon calcium imaging in oligodendrocytes
- Activity-dependent Ca2+ dynamics measurement in OLs during axonal stimulation
- Metabolite flux analysis in myelinated axons
- OL-specific Kir4.1 conditional knockout mouse model
- Immunohistochemistry for GLUT1 and MCT1 transporter expression in myelin
