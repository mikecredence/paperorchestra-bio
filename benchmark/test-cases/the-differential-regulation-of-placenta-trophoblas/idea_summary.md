## Working title

Differential Regulation of Placental Trophoblast Bisphosphoglycerate Mutase in Fetal Growth Restriction

## Core question

What molecular mechanisms in the placenta underlie fetal growth restriction (FGR), and specifically, how does maternal gestational hypoxia alter placental BPGM/2,3-BPG metabolism to contribute to FGR?

## Motivation / gap

- FGR increases perinatal morbidity and mortality, but the molecular drivers remain poorly understood
- Chronic maternal gestational hypoxia and placental insufficiency are known risk factors, yet the downstream signaling is unclear
- The role of bisphosphoglycerate mutase (BPGM) and 2,3-BPG -- key regulators of oxygen affinity in red blood cells -- has not been studied in the placental trophoblast context
- No integrated preclinical-to-human translational study exists linking hypoxia, BPGM regulation, and placental morphology in FGR

## Core contribution

- Establish murine models of acute and chronic gestational hypoxia that recapitulate FGR phenotype
- Show that gestational hypoxia decreases labyrinth area, increases RBC accumulation in the labyrinth, and expands spiral artery diameter
- Demonstrate altered hemoglobin and BPGM/2,3-BPG levels in hypoxic placentae
- Validate findings in human FGR placentae using IHC and metabolomics
- Integrate MR imaging, immunohistochemistry, and metabolomics for a multi-modal characterization

## Method in brief

Case-control design in both murine and human placentae. Mice exposed to acute and chronic gestational hypoxia vs. normoxic controls. Endpoints measured via MR imaging (placental structure), immunohistochemistry (BPGM, hemoglobin), and metabolomics (2,3-BPG levels). Human FGR and control placentae examined with IHC and metabolomic profiling to confirm translational relevance.
