## Experiment 2: Seasonal synchrony between migration phases and virus spread

| Season | Migration Phase | Virus Lineage Movements (n) | % of Annual Movements |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spring (Mar-May) | Northward migration | 42 | 36.8 |
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | Breeding grounds | 12 | 10.5 |
| Autumn (Sep-Nov) | Southward migration | 48 | 42.1 |
| Winter (Dec-Feb) | Wintering grounds | 12 | 10.5 |

Peak virus dispersal aligns with autumn (42.1%) and spring (36.8%) migration periods.

## Notes

- Autumn migration is the single largest driver of intercontinental virus dispersal.
- Anseriformes (waterfowl) are the dominant reservoir and vector, consistent with known ecology.
- The synchrony analysis provides a predictive framework: we can forecast high-risk dispersal windows based on migration calendars.
- Since 2020, unprecedented geographic expansion including South America and Antarctica.
