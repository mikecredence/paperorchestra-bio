## Working title

Synchrony of Bird Migration with Avian Influenza Global Spread; Implications for Vulnerable Bird Orders

## Core question

How does seasonal bird migration facilitate the global dispersal of highly pathogenic avian influenza (HPAI) H5 clade 2.3.4.4, and which avian species and regions are most at risk?

## Motivation / gap

- HPAI H5 clade 2.3.4.4 has caused devastating worldwide outbreaks in poultry, occasional human spillover, and increasing wild bird deaths since 2014.
- Wild bird migration is acknowledged as contributing to global HPAI dispersal, but the mechanistic link between seasonal migration timing and virus spread is unclear.
- We do not know which specific avian species are exposed to these viruses and where, limiting our ability to predict and prevent outbreaks.

## Core contribution

- Demonstrate through phylogeographic analysis that seasonal bird migration explains key features of global HPAI H5 clade 2.3.4.4 dispersal
- Detect synchrony between seasonality of bird annual cycle phases and virus lineage movements
- Identify which avian orders and species are most exposed and vulnerable based on empirical tracking and genomic data
- Integrate bird movement tracking data with virus genome sequences for a phylodynamic framework

## Method in brief

- Viral phylogeography using HPAI H5 clade 2.3.4.4 genome sequences with spatial and temporal metadata
- Bird migration network construction from empirical movement tracking data
- Phylodynamic analysis linking virus lineage movements to bird migration routes and timing
- Seasonal synchrony analysis between bird annual cycle phases and virus spread events
- Species-level exposure risk assessment across avian orders
