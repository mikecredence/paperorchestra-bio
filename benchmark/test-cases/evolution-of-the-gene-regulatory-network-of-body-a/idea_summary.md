## Working title

Enhancer hijacking rewires the Nodal signaling GRN for body axis formation in amphioxus

## Core question

How has the gene regulatory network (GRN) controlling body axis formation via Nodal signaling been rewired in cephalochordate amphioxus, and what is the molecular mechanism underlying this evolutionary change?

## Motivation / gap

- A central goal of evo-devo is to decipher evolutionary patterns of GRNs controlling morphogenesis
- The Nodal signaling GRN (Nodal, Gdf1/3, Lefty) is conserved across deuterostomes for body axis specification
- Despite conserved function, the expression patterns of Gdf1/3 and Nodal are derived in amphioxus, suggesting lineage-specific GRN rewiring
- The molecular mechanism of this GRN rewiring (how expression patterns shifted) is unknown

## Core contribution

- Amphioxus Gdf1/3 orthologue shows nearly no expression during embryogenesis; its duplicate Gdf1/3-like (linked to Lefty) is zygotically expressed in a Lefty-like pattern
- Mutant and transgenic analyses reveal Gdf1/3 is no longer crucial for amphioxus axis formation
- Gdf1/3-like has been co-opted via enhancer hijacking from the neighboring Lefty locus
- Demonstrates enhancer hijacking as a molecular mechanism for GRN evolution in body axis patterning

## Method in brief

- Expression analysis of Gdf1/3, Gdf1/3-like, Lefty, and Nodal during amphioxus embryogenesis
- CRISPR/Cas9 mutant generation for Gdf1/3 and Gdf1/3-like to test functional requirements
- Transgenic reporter assays to map regulatory elements and identify enhancer hijacking
- Comparative genomic analysis of the Gdf1/3-Lefty locus across deuterostomes
