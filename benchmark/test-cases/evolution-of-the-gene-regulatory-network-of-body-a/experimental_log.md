# Experimental Log: Enhancer Hijacking in Amphioxus Body Axis GRN

## Gene expression during amphioxus embryogenesis

| Gene | Zygotic expression | Pattern | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gdf1/3 | Nearly absent | Minimal/none | Ancestral orthologue silenced |
| Gdf1/3-like | Yes (zygotic) | Similar to Lefty | Duplicate gene, co-opted |
| Lefty | Yes (zygotic) | Canonical left-right + axis | Conserved expression |
| Nodal | Yes | Derived pattern in amphioxus | Rewired upstream |

## Mutant phenotype analysis

| Genotype | Axis formation | Phenotype severity | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gdf1/3 knockout | Normal | No defect | Gdf1/3 no longer essential |
| Gdf1/3-like knockout | Disrupted | Severe axis defects | Gdf1/3-like has taken over function |
| Lefty knockout | Disrupted | Expanded Nodal signaling | Conserved negative feedback |
| Wild-type | Normal | Reference | -- |

## Enhancer hijacking evidence

| Evidence type | Finding |
|---|---|
| Genomic linkage | Gdf1/3-like is physically linked to Lefty locus |
| Transgenic reporter | Lefty enhancer drives Gdf1/3-like expression pattern |
| Comparative genomics | Linkage is derived in amphioxus; absent in other deuterostomes |
| Expression correlation | Gdf1/3-like and Lefty share spatial/temporal expression |

## GRN comparison across deuterostomes

| Species group | Gdf1/3 role | Gdf1/3-like | Enhancer hijacking |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vertebrates | Essential for axis | Absent (no duplication) | No |
| Sea urchins | Essential for axis | Absent | No |
| Amphioxus | Dispensable | Essential (co-opted) | Yes (from Lefty enhancer) |

## Notes

- 2024-01-10: Expression profiling confirms Gdf1/3 is nearly silent in amphioxus embryos.
- 2024-01-30: Gdf1/3 CRISPR knockouts develop normally -- function has shifted to Gdf1/3-like.
- 2024-02-14: Transgenic reporters confirm Lefty enhancer drives Gdf1/3-like expression.
- 2024-03-05: Comparative genomics shows the Gdf1/3-like-Lefty linkage is amphioxus-specific.
- Key finding: enhancer hijacking from a neighboring gene is the molecular mechanism for GRN rewiring in body axis specification.
