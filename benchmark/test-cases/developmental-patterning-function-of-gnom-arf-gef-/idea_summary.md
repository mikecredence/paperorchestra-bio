## Working title

GNOM ARF-GEF acts from the plasma membrane, not the Golgi, to execute its developmental patterning function

## Core question

Does the developmental patterning function of GNOM ARF-GEF operate primarily from the plasma membrane rather than the Golgi apparatus, and is it distinct from clathrin-mediated endocytosis?

## Motivation / gap

- GNOM is one of the best-studied trafficking regulators in plants with unique developmental roles in patterning and polarity
- Current models place GN primarily at the Golgi apparatus for secretion/recycling and at the plasma membrane for clathrin-mediated endocytosis (CME)
- The mechanistic basis of GN's unique developmental function (distinct from its homologue GNL1 and other ARF-GEFs) remains elusive
- It is unclear whether GN's developmental role requires its Golgi localization or its PM localization

## Core contribution

- GN localizes to the PM at long-lived structures distinct from clathrin-coated pits; GNL1 does not
- CME and secretion proceed normally in gn knockouts, showing GN is dispensable for these processes
- The GNfewerroots mutant, absent from the Golgi, demonstrates that PM is the major site of GN action for developmental function
- Brefeldin A inhibition experiments redefine the site of GN function
- Fundamentally redefines current notions of GN trafficking function

## Method in brief

- Live-cell imaging to compare GN vs. GNL1 localization at PM structures and clathrin-coated pits
- Analysis of gn knockout lines for CME and secretion phenotypes
- Characterization of the GNfewerroots mutant (GA-absent) for developmental patterning
- Brefeldin A treatment to probe GN dynamics and functional site
