## Working title

Thiolutin Is a Direct Inhibitor of RNA Polymerase II

## Core question

Does thiolutin directly inhibit RNA Polymerase II transcription, or is its in vivo effect on transcription purely indirect through metal chelation and oxidative stress?

## Motivation / gap

- Thiolutin is a natural product dithiolopyrrolone used widely as a transcription inhibitor, but its mechanism of action has been debated for decades
- Previous studies concluded that Pol II inhibition in vivo is indirect, mediated by Zn2+ chelation
- Thiolutin and related compound holomycin chelate Zn2+, but whether this accounts for all biological activity is unclear
- No systematic genetic or biochemical dissection of thiolutin's targets in yeast has been performed
- The role of redox state and metals beyond Zn2+ has not been explored

## Core contribution

- Chemicogenetic screen in S. cerevisiae identifies mutants altering thiolutin sensitivity
- Genetic evidence that thiolutin causes oxidation of thioredoxins in vivo
- Demonstration that thiolutin interacts functionally with multiple metals (Mn2+, Cu2+), not just Zn2+
- Direct in vitro inhibition of Pol II transcription initiation by thiolutin
- Inhibition requires Mn2+ and appropriate reduction conditions; excess DTT abrogates the effect
- Reconciliation with classical literature showing direct transcription inhibition in vitro

## Method in brief

Chemicogenetic screening in S. cerevisiae using deletion/overexpression libraries to find thiolutin-sensitive and -resistant mutants. In vivo thioredoxin oxidation assays. In vitro transcription assays with purified yeast Pol II measuring initiation activity in the presence/absence of thiolutin under varying metal and redox conditions. Dose-response and metal supplementation experiments.
