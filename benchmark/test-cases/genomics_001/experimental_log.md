# Experimental Log -- In Vivo Photopharmacology of Opioid Receptors

## 2024-01-10 -- Compound synthesis and characterization

Synthesized PhOX (caged oxymorphone) and PhNX (caged naloxone).

| Compound | Parent drug | Type | Activity before uncaging | Activity after uncaging |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| PhOX | Oxymorphone | mu-opioid agonist | Inactive | Full agonist activity |
| PhNX | Naloxone | mu-opioid antagonist | Inactive | Full antagonist activity |

Both compounds show clean photolysis with UV/blue light; stable in the dark in vivo.

## 2024-02-15 -- PhOX photoactivation across brain regions

Systemically administered PhOX, then photoactivated locally in multiple brain areas.

| Brain region | Receptor occupancy change | Metabolic activity change | Calcium activity change |
|---|---|---|---|
| VTA | Yes (local) | Yes | Yes |
| NAc | Yes (local) | Yes | Yes |
| PAG | Yes (local) | Yes | Yes |
| Cortex (control, no light) | No change | No change | No change |

Photoactivation produces spatially restricted drug effects -- no change in unilluminated regions.

## 2024-03-01 -- Behavioral readouts

| Behavior assay | PhOX + light (target region) | PhOX no light (control) | Vehicle + light (control) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Analgesia (hot plate) | Significant effect | No effect | No effect |
| Conditioned place preference | Significant effect | No effect | No effect |
| Locomotor activity | Region-dependent | No effect | No effect |

Bidirectional control confirmed: PhNX photoactivation in the same regions blocks opioid-mediated behaviors.

## 2024-04-10 -- Chronic morphine adaptation experiment

Combined PhOX photoactivation with optical dopamine sensor (dLight/GRABDA) in NAc during chronic morphine regimen.

| Condition | DA response to PhOX uncaging in NAc | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline (naive) | Robust DA increase | Normal opioid sensitivity |
| Chronic morphine (day 5) | Attenuated DA response | Reduced opioid sensitivity |
| After withdrawal (day 3) | Partially recovered | Partial resensitization |

This reveals real-time adaptation of mesolimbic dopamine circuitry opioid sensitivity during chronic exposure.

## Summary

PhOX and PhNX enable non-invasive, spatiotemporally precise, bidirectional control of endogenous opioid receptors in vivo. The approach reveals that chronic morphine blunts mesolimbic dopamine opioid sensitivity, recoverable after withdrawal.
