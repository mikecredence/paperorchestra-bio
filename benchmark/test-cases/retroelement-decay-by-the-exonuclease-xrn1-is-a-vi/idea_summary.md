## Working title

Retroelement Decay by the Exonuclease XRN1 Is a Viral Mimicry Dependency in Cancer

## Core question

Beyond ADAR1, what other mechanisms do cancer cells use to tolerate high levels of retroelement-derived dsRNA and avoid triggering a viral mimicry immune response?

## Motivation / gap

- Viral mimicry -- the immune response triggered by endogenous dsRNA from retroelements -- can kill cancer cells or boost anti-tumor immunity.
- Cancer cells paradoxically have dysregulated epigenomes that increase retroelement expression, yet they survive. ADAR1 p150 upregulation is one known tolerance mechanism, but others likely exist.
- No systematic identification of additional viral mimicry adaptation mechanisms and their associated cancer dependencies had been performed.

## Core contribution

- Systematically identify novel viral mimicry adaptation mechanisms by correlating DepMap gene knockout sensitivity with ISG expression across 1005 human cell lines (CCLE)
- Identify XRN1 (a 5'-to-3' RNA exonuclease and RNA decay factor) as an essential gene in cancer cells with high retroelement-derived dsRNA
- Show that XRN1 degrades retroelement transcripts and its loss triggers viral mimicry / interferon response
- Reveal pathways including RNA modification and nucleic acid metabolism as enriched in viral mimicry adaptation

## Method in brief

- Correlation analysis between DepMap gene knockout sensitivity scores and ISG expression levels across CCLE (1005 cell lines)
- Pathway enrichment analysis of top correlated hits
- Functional validation of XRN1 as essential for survival of high-retroelement cancer cells
- Characterization of XRN1 loss: dsRNA accumulation, ISG induction, cell death
- Cross-referencing with ADAR1 dependency data
