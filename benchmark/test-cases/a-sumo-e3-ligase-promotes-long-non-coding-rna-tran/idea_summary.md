## Working title

SUMO E3 ligase Ema2 drives lncRNA transcription for small RNA-directed DNA elimination in Tetrahymena

## Core question

How does the SUMO E3 ligase Ema2 promote the long non-coding RNA transcription that is required for small RNA-directed programmed DNA elimination during sexual development in Tetrahymena?

## Motivation / gap

- In Tetrahymena, Piwi-associated small RNAs (scnRNAs) interact with nascent lncRNAs from the somatic genome to trigger target-directed small RNA degradation (TDSD), and surviving scnRNAs then guide DNA elimination of germline-limited sequences
- The mechanism that actively promotes lncRNA transcription from the somatic genome has been unclear
- SUMOylation is known to regulate transcription in other systems, but its role in lncRNA-dependent genome rearrangement has not been explored

## Core contribution

- Show that Ema2 (a SUMO E3 ligase) is required for lncRNA accumulation from the somatic genome, and consequently for TDSD and viable sexual progeny
- Demonstrate that Ema2 interacts with the SUMO E2 conjugating enzyme Ubc9 and enhances SUMOylation of the transcription regulator Spt6
- Show that Ema2 promotes the chromatin association of both Spt6 and RNA Polymerase II
- Propose a model in which Ema2-directed SUMOylation actively drives lncRNA transcription as a prerequisite for scnRNA-directed heterochromatin formation and DNA elimination

## Method in brief

- Gene knockout / knockdown of Ema2 in Tetrahymena thermophila during conjugation
- Viability assays of sexual progeny
- Co-immunoprecipitation and pulldown assays (Ema2-Ubc9, Ema2-Spt6 interactions)
- In vivo SUMOylation assays for Spt6
- Chromatin immunoprecipitation (ChIP) for Spt6 and RNA Pol II occupancy
- Northern blots / RT-qPCR for lncRNA accumulation
- Small RNA sequencing to monitor scnRNA populations and TDSD
