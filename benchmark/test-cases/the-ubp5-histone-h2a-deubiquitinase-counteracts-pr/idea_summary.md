## Working title

UBP5 Histone H2A Deubiquitinase Counteracts PRC2-Mediated Repression to Regulate Arabidopsis Development and Stress Responses

## Core question

How does the newly identified PRC2 interactor UBP5 modulate H2A monoubiquitination and crosstalk with H3K27me3 to regulate gene expression, plant development, and stress responses in Arabidopsis?

## Motivation / gap

- Polycomb Repressive Complexes (PRC1/PRC2) are central to gene repression via H2Aub and H3K27me3, but PRC-interacting proteins and their functional interplay remain poorly characterized
- Limited understanding of how H2A deubiquitination is coordinated with PRC2 activity in plants
- No known direct interactor linking H2A deubiquitination to PRC2 recruitment in Arabidopsis
- The consequences of disrupting this interplay on development and stress tolerance are unexplored

## Core contribution

- Identification of UBP5 as a novel interactor of PRC2 subunit SWINGER and associated factor PWO1
- Generation and functional characterization of ubp5 CRISPR-Cas9 mutant plants
- Demonstration that UBP5 promotes H2A monoubiquitination erasure, leading to transcriptional de-repression
- Evidence that UBP5 preferentially associates at PRC2 recruiting motifs
- Observation that H3K27me3 increases locally in ubp5 mutants, suggesting functional interplay between UBP5 and PRC2
- UBP5 regulates both development and stress responses

## Method in brief

Protein interaction screens (IP-MS) to identify UBP5 as PRC2 interactor. CRISPR-Cas9 knockout of UBP5 in Arabidopsis. ChIP-seq for H2Aub and H3K27me3 in WT vs ubp5 mutants. RNA-seq for transcriptomic changes. Phenotypic characterization of development and stress response. Genomic binding analysis of UBP5 at PRC2 target loci.
