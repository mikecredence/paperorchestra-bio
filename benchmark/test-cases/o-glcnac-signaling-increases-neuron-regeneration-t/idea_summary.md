## Working title

O-GlcNAc Signaling Increases Neuron Regeneration Through One-Carbon Metabolism in *C. elegans*

## Core question

How does O-GlcNAc transferase (ogt-1) mutation enhance neuron regeneration, and what specific metabolic pathway mediates this effect?

## Motivation / gap

- We know cellular metabolism is crucial for neuron regrowth after injury, but the specific metabolic pathways driving regeneration are poorly mapped.
- Our prior work showed that modulating O-GlcNAc (a nutrient-sensing post-translational modification) can boost in vivo neuron regeneration, but the downstream metabolic mechanism was unclear.
- Nobody has connected O-GlcNAc signaling to one-carbon metabolism (OCM) or the transsulfuration pathway (TSP) in the context of axon regrowth.

## Core contribution

- Define the metabolic route by which ogt-1 mutation drives regeneration: enhanced glycolysis is diverted toward one-carbon metabolism and the downstream transsulfuration pathway
- Show that genetic and pharmacological disruption of OCM or TSP abolishes the regeneration benefit
- Demonstrate this using in vivo laser axotomy and single-neuron regeneration measurements in *C. elegans*
- Link a nutrient-sensing PTM (O-GlcNAc) to a specific metabolic flux that supports neural repair

## Method in brief

- In vivo laser axotomy on individual neurons in *C. elegans*
- Measurement of regenerative outgrowth following axotomy in ogt-1 mutants vs. controls
- Genetic disruption of OCM and TSP pathway genes to test necessity
- Pharmacological inhibition of OCM/TSP enzymes to confirm results
- Metabolic flux analysis connecting glycolysis to one-carbon metabolism
