## Working title

Time-, Tissue- and Treatment-Associated Heterogeneity in Tumour-Residing Migratory Dendritic Cells

## Core question

What are the spatio-temporal dynamics and functional heterogeneity of mRegDCs (mature regulatory DCs) in tumours versus draining lymph nodes, and how does anti-PD-L1 treatment alter their biology?

## Motivation / gap

- CCR7+ mRegDCs are known to migrate from tumours to draining lymph nodes, but a subset remains tumour-resident despite expressing CCR7 -- this is poorly understood
- mRegDCs express regulatory molecules (PD-L1) and are implicated in anti-tumour immunity, but their heterogeneity and temporal evolution are unclear
- How prolonged tumour dwell-time affects mRegDC phenotype and function has not been tracked in real time
- The spatial relationship between tumour mRegDCs and CD8+ T cells, and how checkpoint therapy alters it, is not established

## Core contribution

- Use of photoconvertible mice to precisely track DC migration kinetics
- Discovery that mRegDCs are the dominant population arriving in dLN, but a subset remains tumour-resident
- Tumour-retained mRegDCs are phenotypically and transcriptionally distinct from dLN counterparts
- Progressive reduction in antigen presentation and pro-inflammatory transcripts with longer tumour dwell-time
- Spatial co-localization of tumour mRegDCs with PD-1+CD8+ T cells in human and murine tumours
- Anti-PD-L1 treatment alters tumour mRegDC biology

## Method in brief

Photoconvertible (Kaede or KikGR) mouse models with implanted solid tumours. Photoconversion to time-stamp DCs and track migration from tumour to dLN. Flow cytometry and scRNA-seq to profile mRegDC heterogeneity by location and dwell-time. Spatial transcriptomics / multiplexed IHC to assess co-localization with CD8+ T cells. Anti-PD-L1 treatment arm to evaluate immunotherapy effects.
