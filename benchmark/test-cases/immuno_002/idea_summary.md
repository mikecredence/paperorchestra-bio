## Working title

Influence of B cell-lineage targeted CAR-T cell therapy on humoral immunity and vaccine-induced antibody response

## Core question

How does CAR-T cell therapy targeting B cell lineage antigens (CD19, CD20, BCMA) affect pre-existing pathogen-specific humoral immunity and the capacity to mount vaccine-induced antibody responses?

## Motivation / gap

- CAR-T cell therapy targeting CD19, CD20, or BCMA effectively treats hematologic malignancies but depletes normal B cells and/or plasma cells
- The consequences for pre-existing humoral immunity against vaccine-preventable pathogens are poorly characterized
- It is unclear whether and when patients can be successfully revaccinated after CAR-T therapy
- Systematic longitudinal data on antibody kinetics for multiple pathogens before and after CAR-T are lacking

## Core contribution

- Prospectively evaluate pathogen-specific humoral immunity for 12 vaccine-preventable pathogens in 100 CD19/CD20-CAR-T and 28 BCMA-CAR-T recipients up to one year post-therapy
- Apply comprehensive high-throughput antibody profiling to characterize antibody kinetics
- Demonstrate that pathogen-specific humoral immunity does not significantly change overall after CAR-T, but seroprotective antibodies are absent for up to one-third of pathogens in CD19/CD20-CAR-T and nearly half in BCMA-CAR-T recipients at one year
- Evaluate post-CAR-T vaccine responses in a subset of 72 participants, showing that B-cell reconstitution is critical for effective revaccination
- Support systematic antibody titer assessment and timed revaccination strategies guided by B-cell recovery

## Method in brief

A prospective cohort study enrolling 100 CD19/CD20-CAR-T and 28 BCMA-CAR-T recipients, with serial serum sampling before and up to one year after therapy. Antibodies against 12 vaccine-preventable pathogens were measured using validated serological assays and comprehensive high-throughput antibody profiling. A subset of 72 participants received post-CAR-T vaccinations, and antibody responses were correlated with B-cell reconstitution kinetics assessed by flow cytometry.
