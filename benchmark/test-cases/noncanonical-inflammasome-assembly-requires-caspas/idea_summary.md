## Working title

Noncanonical Inflammasome Assembly Requires Caspase-11 Catalytic Activity and Intra-Molecular Autoprocessing

## Core question

Does caspase-11 form LPS-induced specks (supramolecular organizing centers) in macrophages, and is its catalytic activity and autoprocessing required for noncanonical inflammasome assembly and pyroptosis?

## Motivation / gap

- Inflammasomes are large oligomeric complexes that activate inflammatory caspases via proximity-induced autoprocessing
- Caspase-11 (Casp11) is the cytosolic sensor for bacterial LPS and drives noncanonical inflammasome signaling and pyroptosis via gasdermin D cleavage
- While Casp11 is known to aggregate into a high molecular weight complex upon LPS binding, whether it forms visible SMOC-like specks in cells and whether catalytic activity is required for this assembly are unknown

## Core contribution

- Direct visualization of LPS-induced Casp11 speck formation in macrophages using fluorescently labeled Casp11
- Unexpected finding: catalytic activity of Casp11 is required for speck formation (not just downstream cleavage)
- Intra-molecular autoprocessing of Casp11 is necessary for noncanonical inflammasome assembly
- Revises the model: Casp11 is not a passive passenger recruited to a pre-formed complex; its enzymatic activity actively drives inflammasome oligomerization

## Method in brief

We will generate fluorescently tagged Casp11 constructs (wild-type and catalytic-dead mutants) and express them in macrophages. LPS will be transfected into the cytosol to trigger noncanonical inflammasome activation. Speck formation will be monitored by live-cell fluorescence microscopy. Catalytic mutants (C254A) and autoprocessing-deficient mutants will be tested for speck formation, gasdermin D cleavage, and pyroptosis. Biochemical assays (Western blot, size-exclusion chromatography) will characterize the oligomeric state. Gasdermin D cleavage and cell death (LDH release, propidium iodide uptake) will serve as functional readouts.
