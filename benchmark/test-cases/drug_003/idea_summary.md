## Working title

Proteomic prediction of diverse incident diseases: a machine learning-guided biomarker discovery study

## Core question

Can serum proteomics combined with machine learning improve risk prediction for a broad range of incident diseases beyond what is achievable with conventional health data and polygenic risk scores?

## Motivation / gap

- Disease risk prediction typically relies on clinical variables and, increasingly, polygenic risk scores, but these capture limited biological variation
- The serum proteome reflects real-time biological states and may contain informative disease-predictive signals not captured by genomics alone
- Broad-capture proteomic profiling across multiple diseases simultaneously has not been systematically evaluated for predictive utility
- It is unknown whether a single common sparse protein signature can predict multiple diseases of diverse causes

## Core contribution

- Use a machine learning framework to derive sparse predictive protein models for 23 individual diseases and all-cause premature mortality from 2,923 serum proteins measured on the Olink Explore platform
- Evaluate whether proteomic models improve prediction over health-derived information and polygenic risk scores across 24 diverse outcomes
- Derive a single common sparse multimorbidity signature predictive across multiple diseases
- Demonstrate external replication in the EPIC-Norfolk cohort for six diseases, showing good generalizability
- Identify diseases that benefit most from proteomic approaches

## Method in brief

The study uses nested case-cohort designs within the EPIC-Norfolk prospective cohort (participants aged 40-79, European ancestry, recruited 1993-97, >32,974 person-years follow-up). Baseline serum samples profiled with Olink Explore 1536 and Expansion panels target 2,923 unique proteins. Machine learning models (elastic net, gradient boosting) are trained to predict onset of 23 diseases and premature mortality. Performance is assessed by incremental AUC over clinical variables and polygenic risk scores.
