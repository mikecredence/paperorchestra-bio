## Working title

Deciphering target specificity and inhibition mechanisms of pyrin-only-proteins (POPs) in inflammasome regulation

## Core question

What are the molecular mechanisms and target specificities by which endogenous pyrin-only-proteins (POP1 and POP2) inhibit inflammasome filament assembly?

## Motivation / gap

- Inflammasomes are critical innate immune signaling platforms, but dysregulated inflammasomes cause autoinflammatory disorders and cancer
- POPs were recently identified as endogenous inflammasome inhibitors, but their precise target specificity is unclear
- A previous report suggested POP1 directly inhibits the central adaptor ASC, but this has not been thoroughly validated
- The distinct vs. overlapping roles of POP1 and POP2 in inhibiting upstream receptor PYD filaments vs. ASC nucleation are not well characterized

## Core contribution

- POP1 is a poor inhibitor of ASC (contradicting a previous report); instead, POP1 inhibits upstream receptor PYD filaments (AIM2, IFI16, NLRP3, NLRP6)
- POP2 directly suppresses ASC nucleation AND inhibits elongation of receptor filaments (AIM2, NLRP6)
- Comprehensive mapping of POP target specificity using Rosetta in silico, in vitro, and in cellulo methods
- Design principles for how POPs achieve selective inflammasome inhibition

## Method in brief

- Rosetta computational modelling to predict POP-PYD interactions and binding interfaces
- In vitro filament assembly assays to measure inhibition of ASC and receptor PYD filaments
- Cell-based (in cellulo) inflammasome activation assays to validate functional inhibition
- Systematic comparison of POP1 vs. POP2 specificity across multiple inflammasome receptors
