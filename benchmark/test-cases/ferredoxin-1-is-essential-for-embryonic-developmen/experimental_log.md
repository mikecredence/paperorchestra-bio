# Experimental Log: FDX1 Knockout Mouse Characterization

## Genotype viability

| Genotype | Viability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fdx1+/+ (wild-type) | Normal | Reference |
| Fdx1+/- (heterozygous) | Viable, shortened lifespan | Haploinsufficiency phenotype |
| Fdx1-/- (homozygous KO) | Embryonic lethal | FDX1 essential for development |

## Phenotype comparison: Fdx1+/- vs. Fdxr+/-

| Phenotype | Fdx1+/- | Fdxr+/- | Concordance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shortened lifespan | Yes | Yes | Same |
| Steatohepatitis | Yes | Yes | Same |
| Spontaneous tumors | No | Yes | Divergent |
| Lipid droplet accumulation | Yes | Yes | Same |

## Lipid metabolism findings

| Observation | Fdx1+/- vs. WT | Pathway implicated |
|---|---|---|
| Lipid droplet accumulation | Increased | ABCA1-SREBP1/2 |
| ABCA1 expression | Altered | Cholesterol efflux |
| SREBP1/2 activity | Dysregulated | Lipogenesis / cholesterol synthesis |

## Untargeted lipidomics

| Lipid class | Direction of change (Fdx1+/- vs. WT) | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| Triglycerides | Increased | Consistent with steatohepatitis |
| Cholesterol esters | Altered | ABCA1 pathway involvement |
| Specific phospholipid species | Altered | Detected by untargeted lipidomics |

## Known FDX1 biochemical functions

| Function | Pathway | Disrupted in Fdx1 deficiency |
|---|---|---|
| Steroid biogenesis | CYP11A1, CYP11B1/B2 | Expected |
| Bile acid synthesis | CYP27A1 | Expected |
| Vitamin A/D metabolism | CYP enzymes | Expected |
| TCA cycle lipoylation | DLAT, DLST, GCSH, DBT | Expected (via cuproptosis link) |

## Notes

- 2024-01-06: Fdx1 knockout mouse line generated; homozygous KO confirmed embryonic lethal.
- 2024-01-22: Fdx1+/- cohort established; lifespan monitoring initiated.
- 2024-02-08: Histopathology confirms steatohepatitis in Fdx1+/- but NO spontaneous tumors (unlike Fdxr+/-).
- 2024-02-25: Untargeted lipidomics reveals triglyceride accumulation and specific lipid alterations.
- 2024-03-10: ABCA1-SREBP1/2 pathway analysis suggests mechanism for lipid droplet accumulation.
- Key finding: FDX1 and FDXR share metabolic phenotypes but diverge on tumor susceptibility.
