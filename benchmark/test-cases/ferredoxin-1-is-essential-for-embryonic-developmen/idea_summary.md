## Working title

Ferredoxin 1 is essential for embryonic development and lipid homeostasis in mice

## Core question

What is the physiological role of ferredoxin 1 (FDX1) in mammalian development and lipid metabolism, and how does FDX1 deficiency compare to FDXR deficiency?

## Motivation / gap

- FDX1 is biochemically well-characterized as an electron shuttle in steroid/bile acid biogenesis, Vitamin A/D metabolism, and TCA cycle enzyme lipoylation
- Despite extensive biochemical work, the in vivo physiological role of FDX1 in development and lipid metabolism has not been explored
- The related FDXR heterozygous knockout model shows shortened lifespan, steatohepatitis, and spontaneous tumors -- but no comparable FDX1 model exists
- Whether FDX1 and FDXR deficiency share or diverge in their phenotypic consequences is unknown

## Core contribution

- Fdx1 homozygous knockout is embryonic lethal in mice, establishing FDX1 as essential for development
- Fdx1 heterozygous mice have shortened lifespan and develop steatohepatitis (similar to Fdxr+/- mice)
- Unlike Fdxr+/- mice, Fdx1+/- mice are NOT prone to spontaneous tumors (key phenotypic divergence)
- FDX1 deficiency causes lipid droplet accumulation via the ABCA1-SREBP1/2 pathway
- Untargeted lipidomics reveals specific lipid species alterations

## Method in brief

- Generate Fdx1-knockout mouse model (both homozygous and heterozygous)
- Characterize embryonic lethality, lifespan, and histopathology (steatohepatitis, tumors)
- Compare Fdx1+/- phenotype with published Fdxr+/- phenotype
- Perform untargeted lipidomics and investigate ABCA1-SREBP1/2 signaling pathway
- Assess lipid droplet accumulation in Fdx1-deficient cells and tissues
