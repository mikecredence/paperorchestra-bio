## Working title

Regional patterns of human cortex development correlate with underlying neurobiology

## Core question

What are the molecular and cellular mechanisms that shape regional patterns of cortical thickness development and aging across the human lifespan, and can these population-level associations be validated at the individual subject level?

## Motivation / gap

- Human brain morphology undergoes complex spatially heterogeneous changes over the lifespan, yet the biological drivers of region-specific cortical thickness trajectories remain poorly characterized
- Normative modeling has advanced tracking of brain development at the population level, but the molecular and cellular substrates underlying these normative trajectories are largely unknown
- Prior studies linking brain structure to neurobiology focused on static snapshots rather than developmental change patterns
- It is unclear whether population-level associations between cortical development and molecular brain organization translate to individual longitudinal trajectories

## Core contribution (bullet form)

- Demonstrate that human cortical thickness development and aging trajectories unfold along patterns of molecular and cellular brain organization using a lifespan normative model
- Show that during childhood and adolescence, cortex-wide spatial distributions of dopaminergic receptors, inhibitory neurons, glial cell populations, and brain-metabolic features explain up to 50% of variance in regional cortical thickness trajectories
- Reveal that modeled cortical thickness change during adulthood is best explained by cholinergic and glutamatergic neurotransmitter receptor and transporter distributions rather than dopaminergic features
- Validate population-level findings in individual longitudinal data from over 8,000 adolescents in the IMAGEN cohort, explaining up to 59% of developmental change at cohort level and 18% at single-subject level
- Provide a comprehensive mapping between neurotransmitter systems, cell-type distributions, and cortical morphological development across the lifespan

## Method in brief

Lifespan normative model of regional cortical thickness built from large-scale cross-sectional MRI datasets, yielding age-dependent cortical thickness trajectories for each cortical region. Spatial correlation analysis between cortical thickness change maps at different life stages and molecular/cellular brain maps from PET-derived neurotransmitter receptor atlases, single-cell transcriptomic cell-type distributions, and metabolic features. Dominance analysis to quantify relative explanatory contributions of different molecular systems. Validation in longitudinal MRI data from the IMAGEN adolescent cohort (N > 8,000). Spin-test permutation for spatial significance testing. Cross-validation and out-of-sample prediction to assess generalizability.
