## Working title

Transcription Regulation of SARS-CoV-2 Receptor ACE2 by Sp1: A Potential Therapeutic Target

## Core question

How does SARS-CoV-2 infection upregulate its own receptor ACE2 at the transcriptional level, and can this positive feedback loop be targeted therapeutically?

## Motivation / gap

- ACE2 is the major cell entry receptor for SARS-CoV-2; virus-induced upregulation of ACE2 could facilitate viral propagation
- The transcriptional regulatory mechanisms governing ACE2 expression after SARS-CoV-2 infection are largely unknown
- No systematic screen of transcription factors regulating ACE2 in human lung epithelial cells has been performed
- Identifying druggable nodes in the ACE2 induction pathway could yield therapeutic strategies

## Core contribution

- Screen of 45 luciferase reporters identifies Sp1 as positive and HNF4 as negative transcriptional regulators of ACE2 in HPAEpiC cells
- SARS-CoV-2 infection promotes Sp1 and inhibits HNF4 transcriptional activity
- PI3K/AKT signaling pathway is the crucial node: activated by SARS-CoV-2, increases Sp1 phosphorylation, reduces HNF4 nuclear localization
- Colchicine can inhibit PI3K/AKT pathway, reduce ACE2 expression, and decrease viral load
- Establishes Sp1/PI3K/AKT axis as a potential therapeutic target

## Method in brief

Luciferase reporter array (45 TFs) in HPAEpiC human lung epithelial cells to screen for ACE2 regulators. Validation of Sp1 and HNF4 via knockdown/overexpression and ChIP. PI3K/AKT pathway dissection using inhibitors and phosphorylation assays. SARS-CoV-2 infection experiments measuring Sp1 phosphorylation and HNF4 subcellular localization. Colchicine treatment to test therapeutic potential on ACE2 expression and viral replication.
