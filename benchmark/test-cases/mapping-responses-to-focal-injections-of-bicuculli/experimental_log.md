- Our approach was to represent all three signals as axes in a 3-dimensional space wherein each point corresponds to an individual time point during our recordings.
- When plotting the measurements across an entire respiratory cycle, this multidimensional representation resembles a looped trajectory as shown in Figure 1D for cycles recorded during the baseline and post-injection periods.
- Indeed, each phase of the cycle (late-E, inspiration, and post-I) could be recognized as a particular region along the 3D trajectory.
- Inspiration, for example, occupies the space where airflow and ∫DIA EMG are positive (Figure 1D: red).
- In baseline conditions, the loop is largely confined to the plane defined by the airflow-∫DIA axes, as the ABD muscles are not activated during resting states (Figure 1E, left).
