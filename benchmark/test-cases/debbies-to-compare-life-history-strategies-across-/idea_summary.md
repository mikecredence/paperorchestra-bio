## Working title

DEBBIES: A demographic dataset for cross-taxonomic comparison of ectotherm life history strategies using DEB theory

## Core question

Can we build a standardized dataset of life history traits for ectotherms that enables cross-taxonomic demographic modelling using dynamic energy budget integral projection models (DEB-IPMs)?

## Motivation / gap

- Existing life history datasets lack standardized parameterization suitable for DEB-IPM modelling
- Cross-taxonomic comparisons are difficult because trait definitions and measurement methods differ across studies
- Many ectotherm species are data-deficient, limiting demographic forecasting
- No single dataset links DEB parameters to conservation status and biogeographical characteristics for a broad set of ectotherms

## Core contribution

- The DEBBIES dataset: eight life history traits for 185 ectotherm species (length at birth, puberty length, maximum length, max reproduction rate, energy allocation fraction, von Bertalanffy growth rate, mortality rates)
- Enables parameterization of DEB-IPMs for population growth rate, demographic resilience, and other demographic quantities
- Technical validation showing satisfactory agreement between observed and predicted longevity, generation time, and age at maturity
- Advantages over existing datasets: (i) easy cross-taxonomic comparisons, (ii) coverage of data-deficient species, (iii) population forecasts under novel conditions

## Method in brief

- Compile eight standardized life history traits from DEB theory for 185 ectotherm species
- Parameterize DEB-IPMs to compute demographic quantities (population growth rate, resilience)
- Validate predictions against observed longevity, generation time, and age at maturity
- Link demographic outputs to conservation status and biogeography
