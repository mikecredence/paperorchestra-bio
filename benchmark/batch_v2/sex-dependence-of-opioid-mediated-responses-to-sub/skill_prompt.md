## Idea Summary

# Idea Summary

## Working title
Sex Dependence of Opioid-Mediated Responses to Subanesthetic Ketamine

## Core question
Does opioid receptor blockade suppress ketamine-evoked neural activity changes, structural plasticity, and behavioral effects in a sex-dependent manner?

## Motivation / gap
- Ketamine rapidly reduces depressive symptoms in treatment-resistant depression, but its mechanism beyond NMDAR antagonism is incomplete
- Clinical evidence on whether naltrexone attenuates ketamine's antidepressant effect is contradictory
- Preclinical evidence is similarly conflicting regarding opioid involvement in ketamine's action
- The role of subject sex in the opioid dependence of ketamine's effects has been minimally assessed
- Opioid-mediated effects raise concerns about ketamine's abuse liability during the ongoing opioid crisis

## Core contribution (bullet form)
- Using pharmacological functional ultrasound imaging (fUSI) in awake rats, demonstrated that naltrexone (10 mg/kg) suppressed ketamine-evoked CBV changes in mPFC, NAc, and LHb in males but not females
- Showed opioid-dependent effects were fully reversed by surgical gonadectomy in males
- Effect sizes (Hedge's g) for naltrexone vs vehicle pretreatment in males: Cg1 = -1.20, NAcC = -0.97, LHb = 0.87; in females: Cg1 = 0.53, NAcC = 0.08, LHb = 0.17
- Ketamine increased PSD-95 in mPFC of males (blocked by naltrexone) but not castrated males or females
- Behavioral sensitization to repeated ketamine (10 mg/kg x5 days) was suppressed by naltrexone in males but not females

## Method in brief
Male and female Sprague-Dawley rats underwent chronic craniotomy with polymeric prosthesis for repeated fUSI. Animals received subcutaneous naltrexone (10 mg/kg) or vehicle 10 minutes before IV ketamine (10 mg/kg) or IV vehicle, with each animal imaged three times (VEH+KET, NTX+KET, NTX+VEH). fUSI recorded CBV at coronal slices +2.5 mm and -3.5 mm from bregma in awake restrained animals. ROIs included Cg1, PrL, CPu, NAcC, NAcS, and LHb. Gonadectomized males were tested to assess hormonal dependence.

For structural plasticity, rats received NTX or VEH then ketamine or vehicle IP (10 mg/kg) and were perfused 24 hours later. PSD-95 immunohistochemistry was quantified in mPFC. For behavioral sensitization, rats received 5 daily IP ketamine injections (10 mg/kg) with or without NTX pretreatment, and locomotor activity was measured in open field.

## Experiment 4: MK-801 Control (Selective NMDAR Antagonist)

| Drug | Opioid Dependence of Neural Response | Interpretation |
|------|--------------------------------------|---------------|
| Ketamine | Yes (in males) | Not purely NMDAR-mediated |
| MK-801 | No (NTX does not suppress) | NMDAR-selective effects not opioid-dependent |

This dissociation demonstrates that the opioid-dependent component is specific to ketamine and not a general feature of NMDAR antagonism.
