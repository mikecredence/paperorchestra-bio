## Idea Summary

## Working title
Diazepam Rescues Cerebellar Learning Deficits in a Fragile X Mouse Model via Threshold Metaplasticity

## Core question
Can pharmacological suppression of neural activity with diazepam reverse cerebellar learning impairments in mice with enhanced parallel fiber-Purkinje cell LTD, supporting the threshold metaplasticity hypothesis for why enhanced plasticity impairs learning?

## Motivation / gap
- Enhanced synaptic plasticity often impairs rather than enhances learning, but the mechanism is poorly understood
- Previous work on MHCI KbDb-/- mice suggested a saturation/metaplasticity hypothesis, but needed validation in a second mouse model
- Fragile X syndrome (FXS) mouse models have enhanced PF-Purkinje cell LTD but the link to oculomotor learning deficits was unexplored with Purkinje cell-specific knockouts
- No pharmacological intervention had been tested to rescue learning impairments caused by enhanced LTD
- The metaplasticity hypothesis predicts that suppressing neural activity before training should reset the plasticity threshold and restore learning capacity

## Core contribution (bullet form)
- L7-Fmr1 KO mice (Purkinje cell-specific Fmr1 knockout) show selectively impaired VOR-increase learning (p = 7.37e-4 for WT; KO significantly worse) but normal VOR-decrease learning
- Behavioral pre-training (VOR-decrease or vestibular-only) rescued VOR-increase learning deficits, replicating findings from MHCI KbDb-/- mice
- Systemic diazepam (0.5 mg/kg IP, 18-24 hours before training) fully rescued VOR-increase learning impairments in L7-Fmr1 KO mice
- Diazepam pre-treatment did not affect VOR-decrease learning, confirming specificity to LTD-dependent tasks
- Low-frequency (0.5 Hz) VOR-increase learning was impaired but not rescued by pre-training or diazepam, consistent with involvement of a different plasticity mechanism
- No baseline oculomotor differences between L7-Fmr1 KO and WT mice (VOR gain p = 0.95)

## Method in brief
Purkinje cell-specific Fmr1 knockout mice (L7-Fmr1 KO) were generated by crossing conditional Fmr1 KO mice with L7/Pcp2-Cre mice. Oculomotor learning was assessed using vestibulo-ocular reflex (VOR) adaptation paradigms. VOR-increase training paired vestibular stimuli (1 Hz sinusoidal, +/-10 deg/s) with oppositely directed visual motion for 30 min; VOR-decrease paired same-direction stimuli. Eye movements were measured via magnetic search coil (implanted neodymium magnets + angular magnetic field sensor).

For the pharmacological intervention, mice received IP injection of diazepam (0.5 mg/kg) and were returned to home cages for 18-24 hours before training, allowing the acute sedative effects to dissipate while the neural activity suppression could reverse aberrant LTD. Controls received saline. VOR gain was measured before and after training blocks to quantify learning. Both 1 Hz and 0.5 Hz VOR paradigms were tested to distinguish between different plasticity mechanisms.

## Threshold Metaplasticity Model (Fig 5)

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Normal plasticity | LTD available at appropriate synapses during training |
| Enhanced LTD (Fmr1 KO) | Spontaneous activity aberrantly recruits LTD before training |
| Result | Increased threshold for further LTD induction; unavailable for learning |
| Pre-training rescue | Behavioral patterns reverse aberrant LTD, resetting threshold |
| Diazepam rescue | Suppressed activity prevents aberrant LTD recruitment, resetting threshold |
| Alternative hypothesis (rejected) | Over-recruitment during training (does not explain pre-training effects) |
