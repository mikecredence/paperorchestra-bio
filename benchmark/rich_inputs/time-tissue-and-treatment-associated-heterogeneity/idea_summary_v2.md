## Working title
Spatio-Temporal Heterogeneity of Tumour-Residing mRegDCs and Their Role in Anti-PD-L1 Immunotherapy

## Core question
Are tumour-residing mature regulatory dendritic cells (mRegDCs) heterogeneous in their phenotype and function, and how does tumour dwell-time and anti-PD-L1 treatment shape their capacity to support anti-tumour CD8+ T cell responses?

## Motivation / gap
- mRegDCs (LAMP3+/CCR7+/PD-L1+) are identified across human cancers but their precise role (positive or negative) in anti-tumour immunity is debated
- It is assumed CCR7+ DCs uniformly migrate to draining lymph nodes, but whether some remain in the tumour is unclear
- No study has tracked the temporal dynamics of mRegDC residency within tumours vs migration to lymph nodes
- How prolonged tumour residence affects mRegDC phenotype and function is unknown
- The mechanism by which anti-PD-L1 treatment affects mRegDC states and their interactions with CD8+ T cells has not been characterized

## Core contribution (bullet form)
- Using photoconvertible Kaede mice, showed that mRegDCs are the dominant DC population arriving in dLN but a subset remains tumour-resident despite CCR7 expression
- Tumour-retained mRegDCs are phenotypically and transcriptionally distinct from dLN counterparts, showing progressive reduction in antigen presentation and pro-inflammatory transcripts with tumour dwell-time
- mRegDCs spatially co-localize with PD-1+CD8+ T cells in both human and murine solid tumours
- Anti-PD-L1 treatment skews tumour mRegDCs toward a state enriched in lymphocyte stimulatory molecules including OX40L
- OX40L-expressing mRegDCs augment anti-tumour cytolytic CD8+ T cell activity
- mRegDC signature associated with improved survival in melanoma, breast, colorectal, and lung cancer (TCGA analysis of 4,045 tumours)

## Method in brief
Subcutaneous syngeneic tumours (MC38, MC38-Ova, CT26) were established in photoconvertible Kaede transgenic mice (C57BL/6 and BALB/c). Tumour photoconversion at day 13 labeled resident cells with Kaede-red, enabling tracking of DC migration to draining lymph nodes (Kaede-red+ DCs in dLN = tumour emigrants) at 5h, 24h, 48h, and 72h post-conversion. Anti-PD-L1 (clone 80, 200 ug IP on days 7, 10, 13) or isotype control was administered. ScRNA-seq was performed on FACS-sorted CD45+ cells from tumours.

Human cancer data were analyzed using TCGA bulk transcriptomes (4,045 tumours), METABRIC breast cancer (1,853 tumours), and published scRNA-seq datasets from CRC (62 patients), breast cancer (29 patients), and melanoma (25 patients). Spatial co-localization of mRegDCs and CD8+ T cells was assessed by multiplex immunofluorescence. Functional assays included OX40L-dependent T cell stimulation experiments using OX40L reporter and conditional knockout mice.
