# Idea Summary

## Working title
Antipsychotic Drugs Selectively Decorrelate Long-Range Interactions in Deep Cortical Layers

## Core question
How do antipsychotic drugs affect cortical circuit function across cell types and layers, and do they selectively disrupt visuomotor integration and long-range communication in specific cortical layers?

## Motivation / gap
- Psychosis involves impaired distinction between externally driven and self-generated neural activity
- Antipsychotic drugs have broad neuromodulator receptor binding, but their cortical circuit-level effects remain unclear
- It is unknown whether antipsychotic effects are layer-specific in cortex
- The predictive processing framework posits prediction errors in L2/3 and internal representations in deep layers, but drug effects on this architecture are untested
- Prior work has not systematically compared drug effects across genetically identified cortical neuron types

## Core contribution (bullet form)
- Demonstrated that Tlx3-positive layer 5 IT neurons most strongly distinguish closed-loop from open-loop locomotion onsets compared to superficial layer neurons
- Showed clozapine disrupted visuomotor integration primarily in deep cortical layers (L5 IT neurons)
- Found clozapine, aripiprazole, and haloperidol all strongly reduced L5 IT inter-areal correlations, while amphetamine did not
- Demonstrated antipsychotic treatment preferentially reduced negative prediction error responses and their propagation from V1 to V2am in L5 IT neurons
- Mapped drug effects across 8 Cre lines targeting distinct excitatory and inhibitory neuron types

## Method in brief
Widefield calcium imaging through crystal skull cranial windows was performed in head-fixed mice on a spherical treadmill in a virtual corridor. GCaMP was expressed brain-wide (AAV-PHP.eB in C57BL/6) or in specific neuron types using Cre lines (Cux2-CreERT2, Scnn1a-Cre, Tlx3-Cre, Ntsr1-Cre, PV-Cre, VIP-Cre, SST-Cre) crossed with Ai148 GCaMP6 reporter. Closed-loop (locomotion drives visual flow) and open-loop (visual replay) conditions were compared. Antipsychotic drugs and amphetamine were administered as single injections. Inter-regional correlations were computed across 12 dorsal cortical ROIs. Statistical comparisons used hierarchical bootstrap with 90% confidence intervals.
