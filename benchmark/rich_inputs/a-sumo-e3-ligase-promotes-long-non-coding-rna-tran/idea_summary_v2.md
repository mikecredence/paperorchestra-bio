## Working title

The SUMO E3 ligase Ema2 promotes genome-wide lncRNA transcription from the somatic macronucleus to enable scnRNA-directed DNA elimination in Tetrahymena

## Core question

How is long non-coding RNA (lncRNA) transcription from the somatic genome actively promoted during conjugation in Tetrahymena, and what role does SUMOylation play in connecting lncRNA production to small RNA-mediated programmed DNA elimination?

## Motivation / gap

- Small RNA-directed chromatin silencing requires nascent lncRNA transcription from target loci, creating a paradox: regions targeted for silencing must be actively transcribed
- In most model organisms (yeast, flies, plants), the source and target loci of small RNAs overlap, making it difficult to separately study lncRNA transcription for biogenesis vs. for effector recruitment
- Tetrahymena provides a unique system where source (germline MIC) and target (somatic MAC) loci reside in different nuclei, enabling clean dissection of the lncRNA transcription mechanism
- The molecular mechanism actively promoting lncRNA transcription from the parental MAC during the critical mid-conjugation window was unknown
- While SUMOylation is involved in many chromatin processes, its role in lncRNA transcription for small RNA-mediated genome surveillance had not been established
- Spt6 is an essential transcription regulator, but how its activity is modulated specifically for lncRNA transcription during conjugation was unclear

## Core contribution (bullet form)

- Identified Ema2 (TTHERM_00113330) as a conjugation-specific SUMO E3 ligase with an SP-RING domain, exclusively expressed during conjugation and localized to the MAC with a parental-to-new MAC switch
- Demonstrated that Ema2 knockout partially blocks DNA elimination (Tlr1 FISH signal detected in new MACs of KO exconjugants) and produces inviable sexual progeny
- Showed that Ema2 is required for target-directed scnRNA degradation (TDSD): in EMA2 KO cells, MDS-complementary scnRNAs persist instead of being degraded
- Found that Ema2 interacts with the SUMO E2 enzyme Ubc9 and promotes SUMOylation of the transcription regulator Spt6 (identified by mass spectrometry of His-Smt3-conjugated proteins)
- Demonstrated that Ema2 is required for lncRNA accumulation from the parental MAC (RT-PCR and dsRNA staining show loss of pMAC-lncRNAs in KO)
- Showed Ema2 promotes the chromatin association of both Spt6 and RNA polymerase II (RNAPII/Rpb3), suggesting a mechanism for lncRNA transcription maintenance
- Importantly, SUMOylation-defective Spt6 mutants did not phenocopy the lncRNA defect, suggesting Ema2 may have additional SUMOylation targets beyond Spt6

## Method in brief

The study uses Tetrahymena thermophila as a model, taking advantage of the separation between germline (MIC) and somatic (MAC) genomes during conjugation. EMA2 somatic knockout strains were generated by disrupting all MAC copies. The Ema2 protein was HA-tagged at the endogenous locus for localization studies using immunofluorescence. DNA elimination was assayed by FISH using probes against the Tlr1 transposable element at 36 hours post-mixing (hpm). Target-directed scnRNA degradation was monitored by northern blot hybridization with probes complementary to MDS (macronucleus-destined sequence) regions, comparing scnRNA abundance between wild-type and KO crosses at 3, 4.5, and 6 hpm. H3K27me3 and H3K9me3 heterochromatin marks were assessed by immunofluorescence.

To identify Ema2 as a SUMO E3 ligase, its SP-RING domain was compared with known E3 ligases (MMS21, SIZ1, Su(var)2-10, PIAS1). Recombinant GST-Ema2 and His-Ubc9 proteins were expressed in E. coli for in vitro interaction and SUMOylation assays. For in vivo SUMOylation analysis, His-tagged Smt3 (SUMO) was expressed in wild-type and EMA2 KO backgrounds, and His-Smt3-conjugated proteins were purified by Ni-NTA pulldown from conjugating cells at 6 hpm and analyzed by mass spectrometry (label-free quantification). The chromatin association of Spt6-HA and Rpb3 was assessed by immunofluorescence comparing wild-type and EMA2 KO crosses.

LncRNA accumulation was measured by RT-PCR using primers spanning MDS-IES junctions (which detect lncRNAs but not processed mRNAs) and by immunofluorescence staining of long dsRNA (using the J2 antibody). Genomic DNA controls confirmed that the target loci were intact in KO strains.
